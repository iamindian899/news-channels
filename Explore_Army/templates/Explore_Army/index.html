Sports and Achievements

 
 

Naib Subedar Milkha Singh in action at 1958 Commonwealth Games in Cardiff

 

Milkha Singh popularly known as “The Flying Sikh” was recruited as a Sepoy in 1951. A track and field sprinter, he was introduced to the sport while serving in the Indian Army. He was the only Indian athlete to win an individual athletics gold medal at Commonwealth Games till 2012. He also won gold medals in the 1958 and 1962 Asian Games. He represented India in the 1956 Summer Olympics in Melbourne, the 1960 Summer Olympics in Rome and the 1964 Summer Olympics in Tokyo

 
 

Honorary Captain Shriram Singh Shekhawat

 

VSM is one of the greatest middle distance runner (800m) of India. He won Gold medals in 1974 & 78 Asian Games.

 
 

Naib Subedar Shivnath Singh

 

Naib Subedar Shivnath Singh of Bihar Regiment was the finest long distance runner India has ever produced. He gave sterling performance at the Teheran Asian Games in 1974 winning gold medal in 5000m with new Asian record of 14m 20.5s. He also won the silver medal in 10000m clocking 30m 49.9s. In the Seoul Asian Championship in 1975 he again won the silver medals in both these events.

 
 

Sepoy Basant Bahadur Rana

 

Sepoy Basant Bahadur Rana of 3/8 Gurkha Rifels qualified for the Olympics at the 2012 IAAF World Race Walking Cup in Saransk, Russia in May 2012, with a B qualifying standard.

 
 

Sapper Anil Kumar Prakash

 

Sapper Anil Kumar Prakash of MEG became the fastest Indian by setting a national record of 10.30 seconds in 100 m at the National Athletic meet in 2005.

 
 

Subedar Pan Singh Tomar

 

Subedar Pan Singh Tomar of Bengal Engineers was seven-time National steeplechase champion in the 1950s and 1960s and represented India at the 1958 Asian Games.

 
 

Sepoy Irfan Kolothum Thodi

 

Sepoy Irfan Kolothum Thodi of Madras Regiment holds a national record in the 20km race walk with a timing of 01.20.21 seconds

 
 

Captain Gopal Narayan Devang

 

Captain Gopal Narayan Devang was a silver medalist in Asian Boxing Championship

 
 

Sepoy Shiva Thapa

 

Sepoy Shiva Thapa of Army Sports Institute, Pune is the youngest Indian boxer to qualify for Olympics

 
 

Honorary Captain Padam Bahadur Mall

 

Honorary Captain Padam Bahadur Mall India's first international boxing gold medallist and Arjuna Awardee of Gurkha Regiment is a legend.

 
 

Honorary Captain Hawa Singh

 

Honorary Captain Hawa Singh was Heavy weight boxer & National Championship for 11 straight years from 1961 to 1972, winning gold medals at the 1966 Asian Games and the 1970 Asian Games in Bangkok. He was awarded the Arjuna Award, India's highest sporting award, in 1966.

 
 

Subedar Major Vijay Kumar

 

Subedar Major Vijay Kumar in Pistol Shooting. AVSM, SM won the silver medal in the individual 25 metre rapid fire pistol event at the 2012 Summer Olympics

 
 

Naib Subedar Jitu Rai

 

Naib Subedar Jitu Rai is winner of Gold Medals at 2014 Asian & Commonwealth Games Subedar Major Vijay Kumar in Pistol Shooting.

 
 

Colonel Rajyavardhan Singh Rathore

 

Colonel Rajyavardhan Singh Rathore AVSM, won silver medal in the double trap event in 2004 Olympic Games. In a career spanning over a decade, he also won multiple medals at the Commonwealth and Asian Games. He was awarded the Padma Shri in 2005.

 
 

Major General Mohammed Amin Naik

 

Major General Mohammed Amin Naik SM of Corps of Engineers is a champion Rower. He has been awarded the highest medal to an Indian sportsman the Arjuna Award for his accomplishments in rowing. He won bronze in 1982 Asian Games (coxed pair) and has got 8 gold, one silver, and one bronze in national championships. He has also won two golds, one silver and a bronze in various international championships in rowing.

 
 

Subedar Tarundeep Rai

 

Subedar Tarundeep Rai VSM of 58 GTC became the first Indian to win an individual men's silver medal in archery at the 16th Asian Games on 24 November Major General 2010 in Guangzhou, China.

 
 

Colonel Balbir Singh Kular

 

Colonel Balbir Singh Kular VSM was part of the India hockey teams that won the Asian Games Gold in 1966 and the Olympic Bronze in 1968 He scored three goals in the 1968 Olympics.

 
 

Brigadier Raj Manchanda

 

Brigadier Raj Manchanda nick named the ‘Old Fox’ and ‘The Major’ was one of India’s most dominant squash player in the late 70s. A national champion for record six times from 1977 to 1982, he was also an office bearer of the Federation, holding at one time the post of vice president. Brigadier Raj Manchanda received the Arjuna Award in 1979-80. He is also the recipient of Best Services Sportsman, which he received in 1980-81.

 
 

Naib Subedar Bajrang Lal Takhar

 

Naib Subedar Bajrang Lal Takhar created history by winning first ever gold medal for India in rowing at 2010 Asian Games.

 
 

Naib Subedar Paramjit Singh

 

Naib Subedar Paramjit Singh holds Limca Book of Records of flying 350-km non-stop in a powered hang glider from Bikaner to Sanderav in Rajasthan

 
 

Havildar Sandeep Kumar

 

The World Record for ‘Handle Bar Riding on a Motorcycle’ under fresh category was established by Havildar Sandeep Kumar, covering a distance of 46.9 Km in 01 hr 27 min and 31 secs.

 
 

CAPT MANPREET SINGH

 

Capt Manpreet Singh World Record for ‘Longest continuous ride standing on the seat of a motor cycle’ is held by Capt Manpreet Singh of Corps of Signals, covering distance of 75.2 Km in 02 hrs 24 min and 12 secs.

 
 

Indian Army Ski Expedition

 

Indian Army Ski Expedition to South Pole 2010-11 was the first such Indian Team expedition to the geographical South Pole consisting of 8 members. The team covered 1,170 Km in 49 days & 10 hours and unfurled the National Flag on 15 Jan 2011, the day we celebrate Army Day

 
 

Army Adventure Nodal Centre

 

Army Adventure Nodal Centre ASC Gaya organized longest microlite expedition in the yr 2010 covering 6515 KM across 13 Indian States in 11 days.

 
 

Lt Col Satyendra Verma

 

Lt Col Satyendra Verma of Corps of Signals carried out the first base jump in the country from a height of 158 meters on Pitampura Doordarshan TV tower, New Delhi on 29 Oct 2010.

 
 

First Rafting Expedition

 

First Rafting Expedition at Siachen was successfully completed by Siachen Brigade rafting team covering 245 Km on freezing waters of Nubra and Shyok rivers in Jul 2013.

 
 

Desert Equestrian Expedition

 

Desert Equestrian Expedition covering 1600 km on horseback, organized jointly by Directorate General of Mechanized Forces and Army Adventure Wing commenced on 26 Jan 2009 & concluded on 25 Mar 2009.

 
 

Subedar Neeraj Chopra

 

Subedar Neeraj Chopra won the Nation’s first ever Gold Medal in Javelin with a throw of 87.58m in Olympic Games held at Tokyo 2020. His achievement would inspire other sports persons to excel and bring laurels to the nation.

 

 
 

Subedar Major Satish Kumar

 

Subedar Major Satish Kumar represented India at Tokyo Olympic Games 2020 in boxing & reached the quarterfinals. He competed in the quarterfinal despite being seriously injured in the preceding bout showing grit and determination.
